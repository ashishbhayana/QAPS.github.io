Our Services

QAPS Services

Clinical Governace

Clinical governance is a framework through which an institution and its staff are accountable for continuously improving the quality of patient care. Appropriate systems and processes are in place to monitor clinical practice and safeguard high quality care. A clinical governance framework entails:
- Empowering medical leadership to be accountable and provide oversight of clinical quality
- Minimising clinical risk through the identification of risks and examination of adverse incidents
- Defining and monitoring standards of care

Medical Architecture

A structure for organising medical jobs which includes not only job titles but also job descriptions and delineation of career pathways. It clarifies responsibilities from one job level to another and aids in succession planning. A coherent medical architecture can aid:
- The recruitment and retention of clinicians creating a stable medical workforce

Policy Development

Administrative and clinical policies are a documented system of principles to guide decisions and desired outcomes. The use of clear and effective policies:
- Allow for the standardisation of staff behaviour
- Guide staff behaviour in the delivery of high-quality patient care
- Help drive continuous improvement

Accreditation

Accreditation is a self-assessment and external peer assessment process used by healthcare organisations to assess their level of performance in relation to national and international standards. Striving for and attaining accreditation:
- Demonstrates to patients a commitment to providing high quality healthcare services
- Help to identify areas that need improvement
- Enhances risk management efforts
- leads to enhanced clinical leadership and ownership of clinical processes

Clinical Communications

Effective communication and teamwork is vitally essential to providing high quality care. Clinical communication focuses on clinical peer-to-peer communication and patient-clinician communication which are known to influence quality and safety outcomes through the patient’s journey. Enhanced clinical communication can:
- Prevent and reduce complaints and claims
- Increase patient and clinical satisfaction in the clinical interaction
- Ehance quality and safety of healthcare processes

Clinical Service Development/Strategic Planning

Clinical service development/strategic planning involves bringing people together to identify an issue or an area for growth in service delivery within the broader service system based on the available data, client needs and the organisation’s strategy. Clinical service strategic planning can:
- identify what services are required in the future to meet patient demands i.e. centres of excellence to improve your marketing position
- aid in the optimisation of existing services

Baseline Assessments

We provide guidance and support in high-priority areas using relevant clinical and operational best practices.

Training and Education

We provide on-site customised training programmes and workshops for precise topics in which you need assistance. We share leading practices and “how-to” solutions with your staff. We nurture and build capabilities through hands-on training and mentorship.

Focused Consulting

We assess the current state of performance in your organisation through key stakeholder engagement, field visits and document reviews. We undertake an in-depth review which compares your services and processes to internationally recognised standards and provide a detailed report of key findings including recommendations and priority areas for action.
